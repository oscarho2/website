About

Oscar Ho was born in 2002 in the UK and is currently studying jazz at the Guildhall School of Music & Drama. He previously studied both jazz and classical double bass at the Chetham’s School of Music, from 2019 to 2021, and was awarded the "Ida Carroll Scholarship" for being one of the most improved string players. During his time at Chetham's, he studied with the great jazz bassist and educator Steve Berry, as well as Rachel Meerloo, a bassist from the Hallé. There he was given the opportunity to play great symphonic works, such as Mahler 8, with the Chetham's Symphony Orchestra and worked with the Chetham's Big Band, led by Richard Iles.

In his younger years, he was awarded the first-place prize at the "Northern Region Music Championship" in 2018 for classical guitar and has won many awards from local music festivals. During that time, he was the principal double bassist for the Young Sinfonia at the Sage Gateshead and also played tuba in the Ellington Colliery Band.

Since moving to London, Oscar has found himself with many new opportunities, working in a breadth of styles. Playing in a range of musical productions, as well as working for function bands and orchestras. At the Guildhall, Oscar has worked with the likes of Norma Winston and Scott Stroman. There he took lessons from Steve Watts, Tom Farmer, James Maddren and many other esteemed teachers on the faculty.
